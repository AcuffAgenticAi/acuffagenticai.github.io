Sample dashboard concepts.

These examples illustrate how Acuff Analytics LLC structures dashboards for both business performance and nonprofit impact. Actual dashboards are customized for each client’s data sources and goals.

Business Performance Dashboard

Client: Small Retail / Service Business

Problem: Reporting lived in multiple spreadsheets, making it hard to see trends.

Approach: Consolidated POS and revenue data, standardized KPIs, and built a unified dashboard.

Key Views: Sales over time, product/category breakdowns, returning vs new customers, and forecast indicators.

Outcome: Leadership gained a single source of truth for performance and inventory planning.

Nonprofit Impact Dashboard

Client: Community Nonprofit

Problem: Impact and grant metrics were scattered across unlinked spreadsheets.

Approach: Cleaned and merged program, donor, and volunteer data; aligned KPIs with grant requirements.

Key Views: People served, programs delivered, volunteer hours, donor trends, and quarterly snapshots.

Outcome: Clearer storytelling for funders and a stronger case for continued support.

Financial Reporting Dashboard

Client: Service Organization

Problem: Budget vs actuals were difficult to track across departments.

Approach: Integrated budget sheets with accounting exports and added role-based access to sensitive data.

Key Views: Budget variance, multi-year spend trends, department comparisons, vendor analysis.

Outcome: Better forecasting and cost control across the organization.

Secure Workflow & KPI Audit

Client: Healthcare / Social Services

Problem: Sensitive data required strict access, but leadership still needed actionable analytics.

Approach: Conducted a data-access review, implemented secure aggregation, and documented the BI workflow.

Key Views: Program KPIs, workflow bottlenecks, audit log summaries, role-based activity.

Outcome: Reduced reporting time and increased confidence in data security and compliance.
